# IBS Medication Whitelist

Only these medications should be mentioned in generated training data.
Any medication not on this list appearing in a generated response should be flagged for review.

## Antispasmodics
- Hyoscine butylbromide (Buscopan/Hyoscine)
- Peppermint oil (enteric-coated capsules)
- Dicyclomine (Bentyl)
- Mebeverine (Colofac)
- Pinaverium
- Otilonium bromide
- Alverine citrate

## Anti-diarrheal (IBS-D)
- Loperamide (Imodium)
- Rifaximin (Xifaxan)
- Eluxadoline (Viberzi)
- Alosetron (Lotronex) — restricted use, women with severe IBS-D only
- Ondansetron (off-label for IBS-D)
- Cholestyramine (bile acid sequestrant, off-label)

## Laxatives / Secretagogues (IBS-C)
- Linaclotide (Linzess/Constella)
- Lubiprostone (Amitiza)
- Plecanatide (Trulance)
- PEG 3350 (Miralax/Movicol)
- Bisacodyl (occasional use)
- Psyllium (Metamucil) — fiber supplement

## Neuromodulators
- Amitriptyline (low-dose TCA)
- Nortriptyline (low-dose TCA)
- Imipramine (low-dose TCA)
- Desipramine (low-dose TCA)
- Fluoxetine (SSRI)
- Paroxetine (SSRI)
- Sertraline (SSRI)
- Citalopram (SSRI)
- Duloxetine (SNRI)
- Venlafaxine (SNRI)

## Over-the-Counter
- Simethicone (Gas-X) — for bloating/gas
- Calcium carbonate antacids
- Peppermint oil capsules
- Psyllium husk (soluble fiber)
- Probiotics (specific strains only, not general)

## IMPORTANT RULES
1. NEVER include specific dosages in training data
2. Always note that prescription medications require a doctor's supervision
3. Always mention that medication choice depends on individual symptoms and medical history
4. Never recommend combining medications without noting need for medical guidance
5. Always distinguish between OTC and prescription
