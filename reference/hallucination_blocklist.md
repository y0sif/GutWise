# IBS Hallucination Blocklist

Common false claims that LLMs generate about IBS. If any generated response contains
these claims, it MUST be rejected or corrected.

## False Claims to Reject

### Cure Claims
- "IBS can be cured" — WRONG. IBS is a chronic condition that can be managed, not cured.
- "This supplement/diet will cure your IBS" — No single intervention cures IBS.
- "IBS goes away on its own" — It can fluctuate but is generally chronic.

### Diagnostic Claims
- Any response that diagnoses a user ("You have IBS-D")
- Any response that rules out other conditions without examination
- "Your symptoms are definitely IBS" — Only a doctor can diagnose

### Medication Errors
- Specific dosage recommendations (e.g., "Take 10mg of amitriptyline")
- Recommending stopping prescribed medications
- Recommending prescription drugs without noting they require a doctor's prescription
- Confusing over-the-counter and prescription medications
- Suggesting drug combinations without medical supervision

### False Associations
- "IBS causes cancer" — FALSE. IBS does not increase cancer risk.
- "IBS causes permanent intestinal damage" — FALSE.
- "IBS is caused by stress alone" — Stress is a factor, not the sole cause.
- "IBS is all in your head" — It is a real, physical condition with measurable differences.
- "IBS and IBD are the same thing" — They are distinct conditions.

### Unproven/Dangerous Treatments
- Colonic irrigation/colon cleanses as IBS treatment
- Extended water fasting for IBS
- Unregulated herbal supplements without safety disclaimers
- "Detox" diets
- Any treatment that promises to "heal the gut permanently"

### Dietary Misinformation
- "Everyone with IBS should avoid gluten" — Only if individually sensitive
- "The low-FODMAP diet should be followed permanently" — Only the elimination phase is restrictive
- "All fiber is bad for IBS" — Soluble fiber is generally helpful
- "Probiotics cure IBS" — Some strains may help, but effects are modest and strain-specific
- Specific food lists without noting individual variation

### Scope Violations
- Interpreting lab results or imaging
- Providing mental health diagnoses
- Advising on pregnancy-related GI issues without caveats
- Making claims about conditions beyond IBS without appropriate hedging
